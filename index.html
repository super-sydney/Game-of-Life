What is this?

Conway's Game of Life is a grid of square cells, each of which can be alive or dead. Each cell interacts with its eight neighbours to stay alive or die according to these rules:

  1. Any live cell with fewer than two live neighbors dies because of underpopulation.

  2. Any live cell with two or three live neighbors lives on to the next generation.

  3. Any live cell with more than three live neighbors dies because of overpopulation.

  4. Any dead cell with exactly three live neighbors becomes a live cell, as if by reproduction.
